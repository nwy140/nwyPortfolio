Introduction
.............................

The scenario given is about air pollution in different countries around the world. Data regarding the pollution of air of 27 countries has been collected. Reliable and valid websites such as knoema.com, Wikipedia etc. are used for the data collection to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the data. The data collection is gathered from multiple countries with varying environmental conditions, thus increases the reliability of the data. The objective of gathering this information is to observe any relationships or patterns between the collected data, hence presenting any evidence to support or refute the hypothesis generated. Remarkably, there are two hypotheses generated prior to the data analysis which is "Higher GDP per capita could lead to higher CO2 emission rate because there will be more vehicles" and "Larger number of registered vehicles could cause the CO2 emission rate to increase as more CO2 would be released".

Gathering techniques used

The type of data gathered is secondary data which has been collected by another person. The source of the data regarding the GDP per capita, number of register vehicles and the CO2 emission rate are from reports research made by the government. The data collection is done using the monitored and observed websites. Thus, this reduces the possibility of gathering inaccurate data which may alter the outcome.

  • Observing

    Data regarding GDP per capita , the number of registered vehicles and the CO2 emission rate by country are obtained from Knoema which is a startup company that provides access to a large number of databases.


  • Reporting

    Data regarding factors that causes the increase in the CO2 emission rates are searched online.

Hypothesis 1

Higher GDP per capita will lead to higher CO2 emission rate because there will be more vehicles.

EXPLANATION: The hypothesis is generated to study whether it is true or false that the GDP per capita of a country will affect the CO2 emission rate. GDP per capita is the gross domestic product divided by the number of people in that country, which measures the value of economic activity within a country.

IMPORTANCE : The hypothesis is important as it can prove that the GDP per capita could affect the CO2 emission rate. That being the case, various steps could be taken to control the GDP per capita so that the CO2 emission rate could be controlled.

RESULTS : The GDP per capita and the CO2 emission rate of each country is obtained from reporting. This data is then compared between one another to observe the relationship between these two variables.

Conclusion
.............................

In conclusion, based on all the analysis that has been constructed, it can be concluded that the hypotheses can be refuted as the GDP per capita and the number of registered vehicles of a country does not affect the CO2 emission rate. This is because a high GDP per capita does not necessarily results in a high number of registered vehicles. Besides that, vehicles are not the only thing that emits CO2 as CO2 could be produced by factories, volcanic eruptions etc. Therefore, the hypothesis is not accepted.
